The Horus Heresy

M30 – M31 · the civil war that made the Imperium

  1. c. 963.M30 – 006.M31

    The First Heretic

    Aaron Dembski-Bowden — 2010 · Original Horus Heresy

    Half a century before Isstvan, the Emperor descends on the freshly compliant world of Khur and razes the city of Monarchia that the Word Bearers had raised in His name, denouncing his most devout son Lorgar for the sin of religion. Shamed and adrift, the XVII Legion begins a Pilgrimage to find any truth that might fill the void.

    Novel · Word Bearers standing 4.35 of 5, from 10 455 readings

  2. c. 001.M31

    Horus Rising

    Dan Abnett — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    Two centuries into the Great Crusade, the Emperor has retired to Terra and named his most beloved son Warmaster of the war that will reunite humanity. Newly chosen for the Mournival, Captain Garviel Loken trades the certainty of war for the harder business of speaking truth to a brother who has begun to need it.

    Novel · Luna Wolves standing 4.26 of 5, from 41 579 readings — the gate through which every reader of the Heresy passes

  3. 001 – 004.M31

    A Thousand Sons

    Graham McNeill — 2010 · Original Horus Heresy

    Censured at the Council of Nikaea for the Legion’s open use of psychic sorcery, Magnus the Red withdraws with his Thousand Sons to the crystal city of Tizca on Prospero. There the primarch peers into the warp and glimpses Horus’ coming betrayal — a tragedy of brilliant minds undone by hubris and good intentions.

    Novel · Thousand Sons standing 4.27 of 5, from 10 635 readings — pair with Prospero Burns; the two accounts disagree

  4. 004.M31

    False Gods

    Graham McNeill — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    After Ullanor, Horus rides the wave of his own glory and his Legion bleeds for it. When a ritual duel on the feral world of Davin leaves the Warmaster mortally wounded, a long spirit-walk through the warp shows Horus a future in which the Emperor has discarded the species he built.

    Novel · Luna Wolves standing 4.15 of 5, from 28 641 readings

  5. 005 – 006.M31

    Galaxy in Flames

    Ben Counter — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    On the dead-grey world of Isstvan III, four Legions deploy in force against a recalcitrant governor. What they cannot know is that the deployment is the trap: from orbit their own brothers burn the loyalists to ash with virus and atomic fire. The Horus Heresy is no longer a rumour.

    Novel · Sons of Horus standing 4.19 of 5, from 24 749 readings

  6. 005 – 006.M31

    Fulgrim

    Graham McNeill — 2007 · Original Horus Heresy

    Fulgrim, primarch of the Emperor’s Children, is the finest of the Emperor’s sons: beautiful, brilliant, devoted to perfection. On the murder-world of Laer, a Legion compliance brings home a daemon-bound sword that has been waiting for him. The blade is patient — and the Legion turns slowly, exquisitely, to excess.

    Novel · Emperor’s Children standing 4.05 of 5, from 17 163 readings

Studien-Notiz · Richtung 05 — Kodex

Haltung: Das „Lexicanum" wörtlich: ein schwarzes Stundenbuch. Blackletter-Display (Grenze Gotisch — moderne, lesbare Gotische, kein Fraktur-Cosplay), Rubrizierung als authentisches Auszeichnungssystem (Zinnoberrot für Ordnendes — so haben mittelalterliche Schreiber wirklich markiert), Gold-Versalien für Ären, Glossen in der Marginale statt Meta-Chips. Asymmetrischer Satzspiegel: Textblock + Marginalspalte.

Aus Philipps Runde-2-Vorgaben: Artwork bleibt als fixe Schwelle (Incipit unten links im Bild), Scroll-Scrim dunkelt zum Manuskript-Schwarz — warmes flaches Umbra, bewusst KEIN Verlaufs-Amber (dokumentierte Abneigung).

Verwirft: Cinzel/Cormorant, Icon-Glyphen, Chips/Badges (→ Glossen), Panel-Chrome, jede Sci-Fi-HUD-Anleihe.

Behält: M-Skala als Rubrik-Zeile über jedem Titel, Era-Gruppierung (als Bücher des Kodex mit Gold-Versal), Datenehrlichkeit („the rubricator has left this margin empty"), Vier-Tool-Kopfzeile.

Kern-Parameter: Grenze Gotisch (Display) + Alegreya / Alegreya SC (Text/Auszeichnung). Umbra #110e0a, Tinte #e7dcc2, ZWEI ererbte Akzente mit fester Rollenteilung: Zinnober #b23a27 (ordnet), Gold #b18d43 (schmückt). Buttons = Hairline-Rahmen, primär Zinnober.
