The Horus Heresy

M30 – M31 · the civil war that made the Imperium

  1. The First Heretic

    Aaron Dembski-Bowden — 2010 · Original Horus Heresy

    Half a century before Isstvan, the Emperor descends on the freshly compliant world of Khur and razes the city of Monarchia that the Word Bearers had raised in His name, denouncing his most devout son Lorgar for the sin of religion. Shamed and adrift, the XVII Legion begins a Pilgrimage to find any truth that might fill the void.

    Novel·Word Bearers·4.35 of 5, from 10 455 readings

    c. 963.M30 – 006.M31

  2. Horus Rising

    Dan Abnett — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    Two centuries into the Great Crusade, the Emperor has retired to Terra and named his most beloved son Warmaster of the war that will reunite humanity. Newly chosen for the Mournival, Captain Garviel Loken trades the certainty of war for the harder business of speaking truth to a brother who has begun to need it.

    Novel·Luna Wolves·4.26 of 5, from 41 579 readings

    c. 001.M31

  3. A Thousand Sons

    Graham McNeill — 2010 · Original Horus Heresy

    Censured at the Council of Nikaea for the Legion’s open use of psychic sorcery, Magnus the Red withdraws with his Thousand Sons to the crystal city of Tizca on Prospero. There the primarch peers into the warp and glimpses Horus’ coming betrayal — a tragedy of brilliant minds undone by hubris and good intentions.

    Novel·Thousand Sons·4.27 of 5, from 10 635 readings

    001 – 004.M31

  4. Legion

    Dan Abnett — 2008 · Original Horus Heresy

    On the dust-blown Imperial world of Nurth, an Army officer fights a colonial counter-insurgency that will not end. Strange men in green and gold — the Alpha Legion — arrive late and seem to know more about the war than the men fighting it. By the close, the brothers who lead the Legion have made the most cynical bet in the Heresy.

    Novel·Alpha Legion·4.17 of 5, from 14 320 readings

    c. 003.M31

  5. False Gods

    Graham McNeill — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    After Ullanor, Horus rides the wave of his own glory and his Legion bleeds for it. When a ritual duel on the feral world of Davin leaves the Warmaster mortally wounded, a long spirit-walk through the warp shows Horus a future in which the Emperor has discarded the species he built. He rises restored in body, broken in faith.

    Novel·Luna Wolves·4.15 of 5, from 28 641 readings

    004.M31

  6. The Master of Mankind

    Aaron Dembski-Bowden — 2017 · Original Horus Heresy

    Five years after Magnus’ psychic breach opened the unfinished webway beneath the Imperial Palace, the deepest secret of the Heresy bleeds into open war. The Custodian Guard, the Sisters of Silence and the Mechanicum fight to hold the warp-poisoned tunnels at the threshold of Terra — until only the Emperor Himself can take the field.

    Novel·Adeptus Custodes·4.22 of 5, from 4 045 readings

    004 – 014.M31

  7. Galaxy in Flames

    Ben Counter — 2006 · Original Horus Heresy

    On the dead-grey world of Isstvan III, four Legions deploy in force against a recalcitrant governor. What they cannot know is that the deployment is the trap: from orbit their own brothers burn the loyalists to ash with virus and atomic fire. The Horus Heresy is no longer a rumour. Let the galaxy burn.

    Novel·Sons of Horus·4.19 of 5, from 24 749 readings

    005 – 006.M31

  8. Fulgrim

    Graham McNeill — 2007 · Original Horus Heresy

    Fulgrim, primarch of the Emperor’s Children, is the finest of the Emperor’s sons: beautiful, brilliant, devoted to perfection. On the murder-world of Laer, a Legion compliance brings home a daemon-bound sword that has been waiting for him. The blade is patient — and the Legion turns slowly, exquisitely, to excess.

    Novel·Emperor’s Children·4.05 of 5, from 17 163 readings

    005 – 006.M31

  9. Know No Fear

    Dan Abnett — 2012 · Original Horus Heresy

    On a single afternoon at Calth, Lorgar’s Legion executes the most devastating treacherous strike of the Heresy: orbital ships gut the Ultramarines fleet, ritualists raise daemons through wounds torn in the warp, and the system’s own star is wrenched out of true. Told in clipped present-tense reports, from sergeant to primarch.

    Novel·Ultramarines·4.30 of 5, from 8 105 readings

    007.M31

  10. Betrayer

    Aaron Dembski-Bowden — 2013 · Original Horus Heresy

    Lorgar’s Word Bearers and Angron’s World Eaters carve a shadow crusade through the Five Hundred Worlds in the wake of Calth. Khârn fights to keep his primarch human; Lorgar fights to keep him alive at any price; and the rest of Ultramar burns to give them the time to finish what they started.

    Novel·World Eaters·4.40 of 5, from 6 149 readings
